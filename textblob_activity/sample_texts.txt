TEXT 1: CLIMATE POLICY SPEECH
================================================================================

My fellow citizens, we stand at a crossroads that will define our nation for
generations to come. The science is clear: our planet is warming at an
unprecedented rate, and the consequences of inaction are catastrophic. This is
not a distant threat — it is happening now, in our communities, to our families.

Last year alone, we saw record-breaking wildfires, devastating hurricanes, and
unprecedented flooding. Our farmers face longer droughts. Our coastal cities
face rising seas. The data from our leading research institutions confirms what
we can see with our own eyes: climate change is the defining challenge of our
time.

But I want to be clear about something. Addressing climate change is not just
an environmental issue — it is an economic opportunity. The clean energy sector
already employs over three million Americans, and that number is growing every
year. When we invest in renewable energy, we are investing in jobs. Good-paying
jobs that cannot be outsourced.

We must also be honest about the transition ahead. Communities that have built
their livelihoods around fossil fuels deserve our support and our respect. That
is why our plan includes a twenty billion dollar investment in workforce
retraining, community development, and economic diversification for coal and
oil regions. No worker will be left behind.

Our competitors understand this. China is investing hundreds of billions in
solar and wind technology. Europe has committed to carbon neutrality. If we
fail to act, we will not only face environmental catastrophe — we will lose the
economic race of the twenty-first century to nations that had the courage to
lead.

Some say we cannot afford to act. I say we cannot afford not to. The cost of
inaction — in lives lost, in economic damage, in national security threats —
far exceeds the cost of investment. Every dollar we spend on clean energy today
saves four dollars in climate damage tomorrow. That is not ideology. That is
arithmetic.

Together, we can build a future that is cleaner, more prosperous, and more
secure. A future where every community thrives, where every worker has
opportunity, and where we leave our children a planet worth inheriting. The
time for debate is over. The time for action is now.

TEXT 2: ECONOMIC POLICY SPEECH
================================================================================

Thank you. It is an honor to address you today about the issue that matters
most to every family in this country: our economy. For too long, hardworking
Americans have been told to wait — wait for growth, wait for opportunity, wait
for their government to get out of the way. That waiting is over.

Let me share some facts. Unemployment stands at six point two percent. Wages
have been stagnant for a decade while costs for housing, healthcare, and
education have skyrocketed. Small businesses — the backbone of our economy —
face a regulatory burden that stifles innovation and growth. Meanwhile, large
corporations pay a lower effective tax rate than a teacher or a firefighter.

This is not the free market at work. This is a system that has been rigged by
special interests and defended by politicians who benefit from the status quo.
We need fundamental reform, not incremental tinkering.

Our plan is straightforward. First, we will cut taxes for working families and
small businesses. A family earning less than seventy-five thousand dollars a
year will see their tax burden reduced by twenty percent. Second, we will
simplify regulations — not eliminate them, but make them smarter, faster, and
less burdensome for entrepreneurs. Third, we will invest in infrastructure:
roads, bridges, broadband, and the digital economy of the future.

Critics will say we cannot afford tax cuts and investment at the same time.
But history proves them wrong. When President Kennedy cut taxes in nineteen
sixty-three, the economy grew by five percent the following year. When we
invested in the interstate highway system, it generated ten dollars in
economic activity for every dollar spent. Smart fiscal policy pays for itself.

We must also confront the threat of unfair trade practices. Our trading
partners have manipulated their currencies, subsidized their industries, and
flooded our markets with cheap goods. This has cost us millions of
manufacturing jobs. We will negotiate tougher trade deals that put American
workers first, and we will enforce them with real consequences for violations.

But economic policy cannot succeed in isolation. A strong economy requires a
healthy, educated workforce. That is why our plan includes expanded access to
vocational training and community college, investments in mental health
services, and affordable childcare so that every parent who wants to work can
work.

The American economy has always been the engine of global prosperity. It has
lifted billions out of poverty and created the highest standard of living in
human history. But that engine needs maintenance. It needs investment. And
most of all, it needs leaders who will fight for the people, not the powerful.
That is the commitment I make to you today.

TEXT 3: NEWS HEADLINES (same event, different outlets)
================================================================================

The following headlines are all about the same event: a government announcement
of a new carbon tax policy. Notice how different outlets frame it differently.

FOX NEWS: Government's Radical Carbon Tax Will Crush Small Businesses and Raise Energy Costs for Millions
CNN: White House Unveils Ambitious Carbon Tax Plan Aimed at Cutting Emissions 40% by 2035
BBC: US Announces Carbon Pricing Scheme in Major Climate Policy Shift
NEW YORK TIMES: Carbon Tax Proposal Faces Uphill Battle in Divided Congress
REUTERS: US Carbon Tax Plan Sets Price at $50 Per Ton, Industry Groups Push Back
THE GUARDIAN: Historic Carbon Tax Marks Turning Point in America's Climate Fight, Activists Say
WALL STREET JOURNAL: Markets React to Carbon Tax Announcement: Energy Stocks Tumble, Renewables Surge
BREITBART: Biden's Job-Killing Carbon Tax: Another Attack on American Energy Independence
AL JAZEERA: United States Joins Growing List of Nations Adopting Carbon Pricing Mechanisms
WASHINGTON POST: Inside the Carbon Tax Debate: How the White House Built Its Climate Coalition

TEXT 4: SHORT STATEMENTS FOR QUICK PRACTICE
================================================================================

Use these short statements to practice individually. Each one takes seconds
to analyze with TextBlob.

STATEMENT A (positive, low subjectivity):
"The new infrastructure bill will create thousands of jobs and improve
transportation across the country."

STATEMENT B (negative, high subjectivity):
"The government's reckless spending has destroyed our economy and threatens
the future of hardworking families."

STATEMENT C (neutral, factual):
"The Senate voted 52-48 to approve the budget resolution on Tuesday evening."

STATEMENT D (mixed sentiment):
"While the unemployment rate has dropped, wages remain stagnant and inequality
continues to grow at an alarming pace."

STATEMENT E (appeal to identity):
"We are Americans. We do not retreat from challenges — we rise to meet them.
Together, we will build a stronger, more united nation."
